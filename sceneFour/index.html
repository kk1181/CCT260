Who is being interrogated now?

The butler admits he had long been aware of the master's financial troubles and had been secretly investigating.

Since he uses the same cologne daily, the scent at the crime scene was simply a remnant from his previous visits, not proof of guilt.

The debt note found in his desk confirms his knowledge of the master's crisis, but he had not disclosed it to anyone; instead, he was gathering evidence to prevent further trouble.

This proves that the butler is not the culprit. He was trying to protect the estate's assets.

The maid admits that the master instructed her to deliver a letter to an antique dealer during her grocery trip.

The letter contained details about the plan to sell the ruby, as the master sought to pay off his massive debts.

The maid obeyed out of fear but was unaware of the letter's true contents until the cat detective discovered it in her basket.

This confirms that the maid was not a willing accomplice. She was merely used by the master.
